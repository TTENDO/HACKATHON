FAQs(Frequently Asked Questions)


Q: How does it work?
A:

The parent or grandparent takes out the policy to provide for the child’s education.... You decide the amount of the contribution Prudential calculates the Sum Assured i.e. the amount payable on death.

Q: How long is the policy duration?

A: The policy duration will vary in accordance with the age of the child.You decide for how long you would like to save – the period should be between 5 and 20 years.

Q: What happens at the end of the policy duration?

A: At the expiry of the policy term, Prudential will pay the Maturity Value i.e the Sum Assured plus accrued bonuses, which will be paid in three equal annual installments (with an option for lump sum payment).

Q: Incase of untimely death or permanent disability, what happens?

A: In the event of the untimely death or permanent disability of the parent, Prudential will pay 50 per cent of the Sum Assured for the child’s education as well as pay the full maturity value at the expiry of the term.


Q: Is there an age limit?

A: The minimum entry age for the Pru EduSave plan is 20 years and the maximum age at entry is 60 years.

Q: What happens if I miss a premium payment?

A: You must pay all of the premiums at the agreed date. It is important that you set an affordable premium.

Q: What happens if I stop paying the premiums?

A: If you stop paying the premiums in the first three years, the policy will lapse and you will not be entitled to any money. After three years, you can surrender the policy and get some money back.
